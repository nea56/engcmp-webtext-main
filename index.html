What is Good Design?

A good digital design should be both functional and aesthetically pleasing. It needs to effectively communicate the intended message while being easy to navigate and use. Simplicity is key—users should intuitively understand how to interact with the design without feeling overwhelmed. Good use of color, typography, and spacing is important to guide the user's attention. Consistency across elements helps build trust, and responsiveness ensures the design works well on different devices.

UX/UI Design Study

In this assignment, I conducted a UX study with three participants to gather feedback on logo designs and refine the final version based on their input. The study was guided by Norman’s Three Levels of Processing (Visceral, Behavioral, Reflective) and aimed to understand participants’ first impressions, behavior, and reflections on the design. I created a testing script, interviewed participants via Zoom, and analyzed the feedback to inform potential logo redesigns. The final step involved presenting the UX study to the class through a Google Slides presentation, showcasing the study process, participant insights, and the data-driven final logo design. This project enhanced my skills in user research, design iteration, and creating executive-level presentations.

Project 4: Cartoon Noor

For this assignment, I analyzed an example of good design and broke down how key design principles—like alignment, repetition, balance, proximity, space, and contrast—were used to make it effective. I selected a design that stood out to me, ensuring it was high resolution, and placed it on a page in Adobe InDesign. Then, I added detailed annotations and text explaining how the designer leveraged these principles to create something visually appealing. This project helped me sharpen my skills in layout design, text placement, and understanding design hierarchy, while focusing on how to effectively communicate design concepts.

Project 5: Magazine Cover

In this project, I recreated a two-page magazine spread (11x17") by studying and replicating the design from an existing publication. Inspired by Plato’s Allegory of the Cave and Deleuze’s ideas on repetition, the goal was to capture the essence of the original design, not just copy it. Using Adobe InDesign, I played with fonts, color swatches, and layout tools to recreate the look and feel of the spread. It was a fun challenge that helped me improve my typography and design skills.

Project 6: Design Resume

In this assignment, I created a visually striking resume tailored for creative roles. Using Adobe InDesign, I applied design best practices and tools like text box controls, type on a path, and text wrapping to build a unique and personalized resume. The goal was to break away from traditional styles and craft a resume that boldly showcases my creative potential. I explored models of creative resumes for inspiration, while focusing on making my design both functional and eye-catching. This project helped me strengthen my skills in typography, layout design, and strategic use of design principles to communicate my personal brand effectively.
